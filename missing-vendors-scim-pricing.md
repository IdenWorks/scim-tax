### The Okta Irony
Okta — the company that sells SSO and provisioning as its core product — charges **$4/user/month extra** as an add-on for Lifecycle Management (their SCIM/provisioning module) on top of base SSO pricing. Their Essentials bundle at $17/user/month includes it, but base Starter ($6/user/mo) does not.

### Fully Opaque Vendors (No Public Pricing at All)
Gong, Outreach, Salesloft, Gainsight, Pendo, FullStory, Vanta, Drata, Workday, ServiceNow — all require a sales call before you can even discover whether SCIM exists or what it costs.

## Notes & Caveats

- **Freshdesk / Freshservice** — SCIM is not native; it requires installing a marketplace app. The marketplace app itself is free, but it is gated to the Pro plan tier.
- **Zendesk** — No native SCIM in the core Support product on any plan. Automated provisioning requires IdP connector integrations (Okta, Entra only) on Suite Professional+.
- **Rippling** — SCIM/provisioning is the product's core IT value proposition, sold as the IAM add-on module. Not a "tax" in the traditional sense — it's deliberately unbundled.
- **Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace** — These act as IdPs (sources of truth) rather than SCIM recipients. They provision *other* apps, not the other way around. Included for completeness since many IT teams use them as the provisioning hub.
- **Drift** — Fully acquired by Salesloft; drift.com/pricing now redirects to Salesloft.
- **Amplitude** — The Plus plan ($49/mo self-serve) explicitly excludes SCIM. Growth requires a sales call. The jump is potentially from $600/yr to $36,000+/yr.
- **CrowdStrike** — Pricing is per-device/endpoint, not per-user, making direct comparison difficult.
- **Ashby** — Uses WorkOS for Directory Sync (SCIM). Foundations plan supports SSO as a paid add-on ($100/mo) but still no SCIM at that tier.
- **Culture Amp** — The only major people platform to explicitly and publicly state it does not support SCIM by architectural choice.
